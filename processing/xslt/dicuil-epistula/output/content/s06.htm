<v De omonymis solummodo in diuersis causis inuentis>[70]

1 |folio 14v l. 8 (P) Stagnum appellando aquam metallumque liquidum in singulari numero omonymum.

2 Sed hoc in plurali deficit omonymum fieri, sicut fori tantummodo nauium in singulari et forus contionandi in plurali.

3 Hoc propter defiecienten numerum iam pluralem, illa ne ob neutrum genus ac terminationem in uno singularem, in altero pluralem.[71]

Notes

  1. ^ Chapter number v cannot be found in the manuscript, but it might be hidden by the paper binding. The chapter break has been placed here based on the content. The shortness of this chapter and the lack of any verse epilogue or similar raise the question if the copy is incomplete, but at least short chapters are not unusual for Dicuil, as evidenced by Chapter iii.
  2. ^ In the manuscript, in altero pluralem can be found a second time after this, in faint script.
